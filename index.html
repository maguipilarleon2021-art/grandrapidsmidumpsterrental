Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the "level load" rule and why does it matter?

For safety during transport, debris cannot extend above the top rim of the container. If a bin is overfilled ("heaped"), we legally cannot haul it because debris could slide off onto the roadway. We recommend distributing weight evenly and calling for a swap if you have more trash rental volume than expected.

2. How do you handle heavy materials like concrete or dirt?

Dense materials like concrete, brick, or dirt have strict weight limits because they can easily exceed the legal tonnage a truck can carry. We often supply specific "lowboy" containers for these materials. It is crucial to let us know if you are disposing of heavy masonry so we can provide the correct waste bin rental in #State to avoid overweight fees.

3. Do I need to be present for the delivery or pickup?

Generally, no. As long as the delivery area is clear of cars, locked gates, or low-hanging wires, our drivers can place the bin safely. We do recommend providing detailed placement instructions during booking to ensure the rubbish container rental is dropped exactly where you want it.

4. Are there items that cannot go in the dumpster?

Yes. Federal and local regulations prohibit hazardous materials such as tires, car batteries, asbestos, liquid paints, and flammable oils. Mixing these into a standard garbage rental can result in significant contamination fees or the rejection of the load at the disposal site.

5. What happens if I go over the weight limit?

Every rental includes a generous weight allowance. If your load exceeds this, there is a prorated overage fee per ton. We provide clear guidelines on what different materials weigh to help you avoid these extra costs during your garbage rental.
